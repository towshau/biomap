## Complete Blood Count (CBC)

### Hemoglobin
- **Canonical Unit**: g/dL
- **General Adult Range**: 11.0 - 16.0 g/dL
- **Male**: 13.5 - 17.5 g/dL
- **Female**: 12.0 - 15.5 g/dL

### Hematocrit
- **Canonical Unit**: %
- **General Adult Range**: 36.0 - 52.0%
- **Male**: 41.0 - 50.0%
- **Female**: 36.0 - 48.0%

### White Blood Cell Count (WBC)
- **Canonical Unit**: cells/μL
- **General Adult Range**: 4,000 - 11,000 cells/μL

### Red Blood Cell Count (RBC)
- **Canonical Unit**: cells/μL (million/μL)
- **General Adult Range**: 4.0 - 6.0 million/μL
- **Male**: 4.5 - 5.9 million/μL
- **Female**: 4.0 - 5.2 million/μL

### Platelet Count
- **Canonical Unit**: cells/μL
- **General Adult Range**: 150,000 - 450,000 cells/μL

### Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV)
- **Canonical Unit**: fL
- **General Adult Range**: 80 - 100 fL

### Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin (MCH)
- **Canonical Unit**: pg
- **General Adult Range**: 27 - 31 pg

### Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration (MCHC)
- **Canonical Unit**: g/dL
- **General Adult Range**: 32 - 36 g/dL

### Red Cell Distribution Width (RDW)
- **Canonical Unit**: %
- **General Adult Range**: 11.5 - 14.5%

## Metabolic Panel

### Fasting Glucose
- **Canonical Unit**: mmol/L
- **General Adult Range**: 3.9 - 5.5 mmol/L

### Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c)
- **Canonical Unit**: %
- **Normal**: <5.7%
- **Prediabetes**: 5.7-6.4%
- **Diabetes**: ≥6.5%

### Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN)
- **Canonical Unit**: mg/dL
- **General Adult Range**: 7 - 20 mg/dL

### Creatinine
- **Canonical Unit**: mg/dL
- **General Adult Range**: 0.6 - 1.3 mg/dL
- **Male**: 0.7 - 1.3 mg/dL
- **Female**: 0.6 - 1.2 mg/dL

### Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate (eGFR)
- **Canonical Unit**: mg/dL (Note: unit may need adjustment)
- **Normal**: ≥90 mL/min/1.73m²

### Sodium
- **Canonical Unit**: mEq/L
- **General Adult Range**: 136 - 145 mEq/L

### Potassium
- **Canonical Unit**: mEq/L
- **General Adult Range**: 3.5 - 5.0 mEq/L

### Chloride
- **Canonical Unit**: mEq/L
- **General Adult Range**: 98 - 107 mEq/L

### Carbon Dioxide (CO2)
- **Canonical Unit**: mEq/L
- **General Adult Range**: 22 - 28 mEq/L

### Calcium
- **Canonical Unit**: mg/dL
- **General Adult Range**: 8.5 - 10.5 mg/dL

## Important Notes

### Cycle Phase-Specific Ranges
Several female hormones have different reference ranges depending on the menstrual cycle phase:
- **Follicular Phase**: Days 1-14 of menstrual cycle
- **Mid-Cycle/Ovulation**: Around day 14 (peak LH/FSH)
- **Luteal Phase**: Days 15-28 of menstrual cycle

When interpreting results, consider the day of the menstrual cycle or use the most conservative (widest) range if cycle phase is unknown.

### Age-Specific Ranges
Most ranges listed are for adults (18+ years). Pediatric and geriatric populations may have different reference ranges. Age-specific ranges are noted where available (e.g., AMH has age-stratified ranges).

### Sex-Specific Ranges
Many biomarkers have different reference ranges for males and females. Where sex-specific ranges exist, they are listed separately. General adult ranges are provided when there is no significant sex difference.

### Sources
Reference ranges are derived from consensus clinical guidelines including:
- **Consensus Clinical Range**: General medical consensus
- **AHA Guidelines**: American Heart Association
- **ADA Guidelines**: American Diabetes Association
- **NKF Guidelines**: National Kidney Foundation

### Canonical Units
All biomarkers have been normalized to a canonical unit. The database supports flexible unit ingestion through unit aliases, but all stored values are normalized to these canonical units for consistency and comparison.

## Querying Reference Ranges

To query reference ranges programmatically:

```sql
-- Get all reference ranges for a specific biomarker
SELECT 
    mt.name,
    bu.unit_code,
    brr.min_value,
    brr.max_value,
    brr.sex,
    brr.age_min,
    brr.age_max,
    brr.notes
FROM biomap_reference_ranges brr
JOIN biomap_measurement_types mt ON mt.id = brr.measurement_type_id
JOIN biomap_units bu ON bu.id = brr.unit_id
WHERE mt.slug = 'testosterone_total'
ORDER BY brr.sex NULLS FIRST, brr.age_min NULLS FIRST;
```
